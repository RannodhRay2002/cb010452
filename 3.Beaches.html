Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna is one of Sri Lanka's most renowned tourist destinations, attracting both tourists and residents to its timeless beauty. With its golden sand, sparkling water, and lush palm trees, this banana-shaped beach is the ideal spot to unwind and spend quality time with your family. You can sit back, relax, and enjoy your day with a drink on one of the sunbeds. The beach is a terrific location to relax after a hot summer day by taking a dip in the ocean and learning about the local culture.

As a result, many Sri Lankan families flock to the beach for a refreshing afternoon dip. The beach is also bustling with stylish and trendy eateries and little shops selling a variety of merchandise and scrumptious meals. While strolling along the gorgeous beach's west side, you can also see the iconic Japanese Peace Pagoda Statue. So, get out to the famed Unawatuna beach, take in the scenery, and unwind while drinking a nice and delicious cocktail.

Induruwa Beach

Induruwa beach, located in Induruwa hamlet in Bentota town in Sri Lanka, is a tranquil white sand beach that is ideal for surfing, sunbathing, and chilling with friends and loved ones. Furthermore, there are a variety of lodging options along this beach, ranging from beach style homes to five-star hotels. The panoramic splendor of the villages, as well as some relaxation hotspots, await visitors at Induruwa Beach. After spending some quality time on the golden sand beach, you will feel relaxed and rejuvenated. When visiting this beach, make sure to wear flip-flops and loose half-sleeved t-shirts. A visit to this beach will not empty your wallet.

• Get some golden tan while sunbathing on the beach, enjoy long walks along the banks of the sea and explore the Bopath Ella fall.
• Induruwa beach has quite a few local markets where you can buy some gifts for your loved ones.
• Visit the sweet water lagoons and the Maadu river to have an excellent view of lush greenery and get some sights of birds and aquatic animals.
• Enroll for a wellness program and Ayurveda practices to rejuvenate yourself.
• Enlighten your senses at the Batatotalena cave and visit the small town of Batugedara for precious gemstones and jewelry.

Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay, a Sri Lankan coastal town on the Indian Ocean, is 320 kilometers from Colombo, the country's capital. Arugam Bay is known as a surfer's paradise because of the numerous surfing locations distributed around the area for both beginners and experienced surfers. Main Point and Whiskey Point are two of the most popular surfing spots in the area, with surfboard rentals nearby. If you don't want to surf, you can relax on the gorgeous beaches. The beaches host numerous beach parties at night, providing an opportunity to soak up the lively atmosphere. Arugam Bay is much more than just a beach and a surfer's paradise.

A stroll through the streets of Arugam Bay in the evening may bring you to food vendors where you may sample the local cuisine. Another highlight of this seaside hamlet is the lagoon safaris, where visitors can see elephants and crocodiles. Kudumbigala Monastery, Kumana National Park, and Elephant Rock are among the other attractions in and around Arugam Bay worth seeing. In general, Arugam Bay is the spot to visit in Sri Lanka if you're seeking for a dynamic vacation.
